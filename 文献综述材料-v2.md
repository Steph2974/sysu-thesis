  The landscape of POI recommendation and next POI prediction has seen a diverse array of approaches aimed at enhancing personalization, capturing complex spatial-temporal dependencies, and addressing challenges such as data sparsity and recommendation diversity. Several studies emphasize the importance of leveraging graph-based models to incorporate geographical and collaborative signals effectively. For instance, Li et al. [1] introduce the Sequence-to-Graph (Seq2Graph) augmentation technique, which propagates collaborative signals across POI sequences by constructing a graph structure, thereby enabling a joint learning of POI embeddings and user preferences. Similarly, Wang et al. [3] propose a Graph-Enhanced Spatial-Temporal Network that explicitly models geographical influences through a graph embedding approach, addressing limitations of traditional RNN-based methods.

  The integration of multi-task learning frameworks to handle data sparsity and improve recommendation accuracy is also prominent. Lim et al. [4] develop a Hierarchical Multi-Task Graph Recurrent Network (HMT-GRN), which learns user-region matrices at different spatial granularities and employs hierarchical beam search to efficiently predict the next POI. This approach effectively reduces the search space and enhances prediction performance by capturing regional preferences.

  Addressing the need for more personalized and serendipitous recommendations, Zhang et al. [2] propose SNPR, a serendipity-oriented model that balances relevance with unexpectedness, thereby mitigating user boredom with overly obvious suggestions. Complementing this, Wang et al. [5] introduce a Disentangled Representation-enhanced Attention Network (DRAN), which explicitly models different aspects influencing POI attractiveness, leading to more precise and personalized recommendations.

  Recent advancements also explore novel modeling paradigms beyond traditional neural networks. Qin et al. [6] present Diff-POI, a diffusion-based model that captures spatial preferences through a diffusion process, offering a probabilistic perspective on user movement patterns. Yin et al. [7] contribute the SNPM model, which utilizes dynamic graphs and explicit dependency modeling to predict user check-ins, emphasizing the importance of temporal dynamics.

  The emergence of large language models (LLMs) in POI recommendation marks a significant shift, as highlighted by Li et al. [8], who propose a framework leveraging pretrained LLMs to overcome the limitations of numerical representations and incorporate richer contextual information. Additionally, Lai et al. [9] focus on modeling cooperative associations between different aspects of POIs using disentangled contrastive hypergraph learning, aiming to better capture the multifaceted influences on user preferences.

  Finally, the integration of global and user-specific perspectives is exemplified by Zuo et al. [10], who introduce GUGEN, a Global User Graph Enhanced Network that combines global POI features and geographical relationships to improve recommendation robustness across diverse user populations.

  Collectively, these studies demonstrate a trend towards sophisticated graph-based, multi-task, and deep learning models that aim to improve the accuracy, diversity, and personalization of next POI recommendations, addressing key challenges such as spatial-temporal complexity, data sparsity, and user engagement.

  The domain of sequence recommendation and POI sequence modeling has seen a diverse array of approaches aimed at capturing user preferences and spatial-temporal dynamics. Early efforts primarily focused on leveraging hierarchical spatial representations to address data sparsity issues. For instance, Hossain et al. [1] introduced a POI recommendation system that employs hierarchical gridding of the map to mitigate data sparsity, although this approach struggles to represent spatial distances smoothly.

  Attention mechanisms have become prominent in modeling sequential POI data. Li et al. [2] proposed an attention-based spatiotemporal gated graph neural network (ATST-GGNN), which significantly enhances the precision and ranking metrics by effectively capturing complex spatiotemporal dependencies. Similarly, Yang et al. [4] integrated attention mechanisms with adaptive convolutional fusion networks to optimize user preference forecasting, utilizing both long- and short-term memory networks to incorporate contextual information such as temporal and categorical features.

  Hierarchical and multi-granularity modeling techniques have also been explored to uncover latent structural patterns within check-in sequences. Xie et al. [5] employed a hierarchical transformer architecture that stacks encoders to capture multi-level short-term structural patterns, thereby improving next POI prediction accuracy. Complementing this, Wang et al. [3] introduced a spatial-temporal interval-aware sequential model (STiSAN), which considers spatial-temporal intervals explicitly through lightweight modules, enhancing the model's ability to handle spatial-temporal heterogeneity without significant computational overhead.

  Graph neural networks (GNNs) have been utilized to model complex spatial-temporal relationships. Li et al. [2] demonstrated the effectiveness of an attention-based spatiotemporal GGNN, which outperformed existing methods in key performance metrics. This approach underscores the importance of explicitly modeling spatial-temporal interactions within user check-in sequences.

  Recent advancements have incorporated diffusion models and contrastive learning frameworks to address the challenges of data sparsity and noise. Qin et al. [7] proposed Diff-POI, a diffusion-based model that samples user spatial preferences, showing superior performance over baseline methods. Zhou et al. [9] introduced CLLP, a contrastive learning framework that models latent user preferences at each POI, leading to more disentangled and interpretable preference representations. Additionally, Qi et al. [10] developed a counterfactual sequence synthesis approach combined with continuous-time dynamic preference modeling to mitigate the effects of data sparsity and noise in sequential POI recommendation.

  Frameworks that incorporate multi-modal contextual information have also been proposed. Wan et al. [6] extended traditional collaborative filtering with a context-aware POI embedding architecture, aiming to better exploit sequential contextual patterns rather than relying solely on auxiliary multi-modal data. Furthermore, Feng et al. [8] introduced a fast recommendation strategy tailored for long user behavior sequences, which reduces computational complexity by avoiding subsequence selection, thus facilitating real-time applications.

  In summary, the landscape of sequence recommendation and POI sequence modeling encompasses a variety of techniques, including hierarchical transformers, attention mechanisms, graph neural networks, diffusion models, and contrastive learning. These methods collectively aim to enhance the modeling of spatial-temporal dependencies, user preferences, and contextual information, thereby advancing the accuracy and efficiency of next POI prediction systems.
